Retail and ecommerce businesses in 2026 operate across more channels than at any point in the industry's history. A single brand might sell through its own Shopify storefront, Amazon and Walmart marketplaces, a network of physical stores, and a wholesale portal, all simultaneously. The operational complexity this creates is not a technology problem you can solve with another point solution. It is an integration problem, and that is precisely what ERP systems are designed to address.

This guide evaluates the eight strongest ERP platforms for retail and ecommerce operations in 2026. Each was selected because omnichannel commerce is a primary design focus, not a bolted-on module. We assess them on the capabilities that determine success in unified commerce: real-time inventory visibility, order orchestration, POS integration, marketplace connectivity, and returns management.

## Why Retail and Ecommerce Needs Unified ERP

Consumers no longer distinguish between channels. They browse on mobile, purchase on desktop, pick up in store, and return by mail. Retailers that treat these as separate operational silos create inconsistencies that erode trust: items showing as available online but out of stock at pickup, returns processed in-store that never credit the original online order, or loyalty balances that only reflect one channel.

**Inventory accuracy** is the most consequential operational metric for omnichannel retailers. Stockouts cost revenue. Overselling costs marketplace seller ratings. A purpose-built retail ERP maintains a single inventory truth and projects available-to-promise quantities across every channel, warehouse, and store location. The best systems account for in-transit stock, reserved items, and allocation rules that prioritize high-margin channels during scarcity.

**Order orchestration** has evolved beyond shipping from a single warehouse. Orders may be fulfilled from distribution centers, stores as micro-fulfillment nodes, 3PLs, or via drop-ship. The ERP routes each order to the optimal location based on inventory position, proximity, shipping cost, and delivery commitments.

**Returns management** directly affects profitability in categories where return rates reach 30 percent. An ERP with strong reverse logistics routes returned items to the disposition channel that recovers the most value, whether that is back to sellable inventory, a secondary marketplace, or liquidation. Without integrated returns, retailers experience days-long delays between receiving a return and making inventory available for resale.

## Top 8 ERP Systems for Retail and Ecommerce

### 1. NetSuite SuiteCommerce

Oracle NetSuite with SuiteCommerce is the most widely deployed cloud ERP among mid-market retail and ecommerce businesses. The platform natively integrates an ecommerce storefront, POS, order management, inventory, and financials in a single database with no middleware layer.

**Key capabilities:** SuiteCommerce InStore POS with real-time inventory lookup, native Amazon/eBay/Walmart marketplace integration, advanced available-to-promise engine, demand planning with seasonal forecasting, and built-in promotion management tied to financials.

**Pricing:** ~$99/user/month plus $999/month platform fee. Implementation typically $50,000 to $150,000.

**Best for:** Mid-market retailers and DTC brands doing $5M to $200M in revenue. See our [NetSuite vs Dynamics 365 comparison](/compare/netsuite-vs-dynamics-365/) for a detailed head-to-head.

### 2. Microsoft Dynamics 365 Commerce

Dynamics 365 Commerce is Microsoft's enterprise omnichannel solution, combining a modern POS, ecommerce engine, and back-office operations. Native integration with Teams, Power BI, and Azure AI gives Microsoft-invested organizations a lower integration burden.

**Key capabilities:** Cloud POS with offline mode, AI-driven product recommendations via Azure Cognitive Services, distributed order management, clienteling tools surfacing customer history at point of sale, and fraud protection via Azure ML.

**Pricing:** ~$180/user/month. Enterprise implementations range from $200,000 to $1M+.

**Best for:** Enterprise retailers with 50+ locations already in the Microsoft ecosystem. See our [Dynamics 365 vs SAP comparison](/compare/microsoft-dynamics-365-vs-sap/).

### 3. SAP S/4HANA Retail

SAP S/4HANA for Retail processes massive transaction volumes on the HANA in-memory database with real-time analytics. The Customer Activity Repository (CAR) aggregates POS, ecommerce, and mobile transaction data into a single analytical layer.

**Key capabilities:** Unified transaction analytics via CAR, ML-powered forecasting and replenishment, promotion management with margin simulation, SAP Commerce Cloud integration, and real-time assortment planning at the store-SKU level.

**Pricing:** ~$250/user/month. First-year cost routinely exceeds $1M including implementation.

**Best for:** Large retailers with 200+ locations and global operations processing millions of daily transactions. Not practical under $500M revenue.

### 4. Oracle Retail

Oracle Retail is a purpose-built suite for tier-1 retailers, distinct from Oracle's general ERP. The suite includes Xstore POS, Retail Merchandising System, Demand Forecasting, and Order Management.

**Key capabilities:** Xstore POS with offline resilience, AI demand forecasting with weather and event signals, science-driven markdown optimization, cross-channel order broker, and supplier collaboration for drop-ship management.

**Pricing:** Custom enterprise pricing. TCO typically starts at $500K+ annually.

**Best for:** Tier-1 retailers with thousands of locations and millions of SKUs. Grocery, fashion, and department store chains needing retail-specific optimization.

### 5. Brightpearl (Sage)

Acquired by Sage in 2021, Brightpearl focuses on the operational layer between selling channels and accounting. It handles orders, inventory, purchasing, and fulfillment in a system designed from the ground up for ecommerce workflows.

**Key capabilities:** Native integrations with 15+ channels (Shopify, Amazon, eBay, Walmart, BigCommerce), automation engine for hands-free order processing, built-in WMS with barcode scanning, demand forecasting with automated PO generation, and POS integration via Lightspeed and Vend.

**Pricing:** Starting ~$375/month, scaling with order volume. Implementation $10,000 to $50,000.

**Best for:** SMB multichannel retailers doing $1M to $50M that want sophisticated automation without enterprise ERP complexity.

### 6. Acumatica Commerce Edition

Acumatica brings enterprise ERP capabilities to the mid-market with usage-based pricing that does not penalize headcount growth. Native connectors to major ecommerce platforms plus a full financial backend make it a strong all-in-one choice.

**Key capabilities:** Native Shopify, BigCommerce, Amazon, and WooCommerce connectors, real-time inventory sync across channels, built-in WMS with pick/pack/ship, usage-based pricing model, and multi-entity financial consolidation.

**Pricing:** ~$1,800/user/year (usage-based). Implementation $30,000 to $100,000.

**Best for:** Mid-market retailers doing $5M to $100M that need full ERP with ecommerce connectivity, especially those with large teams facing prohibitive per-user costs elsewhere.

### 7. Shopify + ERP Integrations

Shopify is not an ERP, but its ecommerce dominance means many retailers build their stack around it. Shopify Plus combined with ERP connectors or middleware (Celigo, Pipe17) creates a composable architecture where Shopify handles the storefront while the ERP manages operations.

**Key capabilities:** Shopify POS for unified online/in-store selling, Marketplace Connect for Amazon/eBay/Walmart, Flow automation for order routing, pre-built connectors for NetSuite/Dynamics 365/Acumatica, and Shop Pay checkout optimization.

**Pricing:** Shopify Plus starts at $2,300/month. ERP and middleware add $500 to $2,000/month on top.

**Best for:** Ecommerce-first brands doing $10M+ that have invested heavily in Shopify and do not want to rip out their storefront. Less suitable when wholesale or manufacturing are significant operations.

### 8. Cin7

Cin7 is an inventory and order management platform spanning B2B, B2C, and retail channels. With 350+ native integrations, it connects to virtually any selling channel, 3PL, or accounting system at a price point accessible to growing product brands.

**Key capabilities:** 350+ integrations (Shopify, Amazon, eBay, WooCommerce, B2B portals), integrated POS, multi-warehouse management with inter-warehouse transfers, EDI for wholesale compliance, and production module for kitting operations.

**Pricing:** Starting ~$349/month. Implementation $5,000 to $25,000.

**Best for:** Product brands doing $1M to $30M needing robust inventory management across channels, especially those with wholesale operations requiring EDI compliance.

## Omnichannel Requirements

Beyond the system-specific evaluations above, any ERP shortlisted for retail must deliver four non-negotiable capabilities.

**Unified inventory visibility.** A single truth for stock across warehouses, stores, in-transit, and allocated to orders. Available-to-promise must update in real time. Systems relying on periodic sync, even hourly, create oversell risk during peak volume.

**Native POS integration.** The POS must be integrated with the ERP directly, not through middleware. Native integration enables endless aisle selling, ship-from-store, and BOPIS without manual orchestration. Middleware-connected POS introduces latency and failure points.

**Marketplace connectivity.** Bidirectional data flow covering product listings, pricing, inventory, orders, returns, and settlement. The best systems handle this natively rather than requiring a separate marketplace management platform.

**Returns as a first-class workflow.** Automated RMA, inspection, disposition routing, inventory re-availability, and financial processing within a single workflow. Returns should not require manual intervention for standard scenarios.

## Selection Criteria

**Match scale to system.** As a framework: $1M-$10M revenue maps to Cin7, Brightpearl, or Shopify + lightweight ERP. $10M-$100M maps to NetSuite, Acumatica, or Dynamics 365. $100M-$500M maps to Dynamics 365 Commerce or SAP. Above $500M, SAP S/4HANA Retail or Oracle Retail.

**Evaluate channel coverage.** Map every current and planned selling channel. Verify that the ERP supports each natively or through a maintained connector. Marketplace APIs change frequently, and building custom integrations is a maintenance liability.

**Assess total cost of ownership.** License cost is typically 30 to 40 percent of the first-year investment. Implementation, migration, integration, training, and support make up the rest. Get scoped proposals from at least two partners and insist on fixed-fee or capped arrangements.

**Prioritize implementation speed.** Brightpearl and Cin7 deploy in 4 to 8 weeks. NetSuite and Acumatica take 3 to 6 months. Dynamics 365, SAP, and Oracle implementations commonly take 9 to 18 months.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the difference between a retail ERP and a general-purpose ERP?

A retail ERP includes native POS, omnichannel inventory, marketplace connectivity, and workflows for promotions, markdowns, and returns. General-purpose systems may offer some of these through add-ons, but the workflows are not designed for retail's high transaction volumes, seasonal patterns, and multi-channel complexity.

### Can I use Shopify as my ERP?

No. Shopify is an ecommerce and POS platform, not an ERP. It lacks financial management, procurement, and warehouse management. However, Shopify integrates well with several ERPs on this list. Many retailers use Shopify for customer-facing commerce and a separate ERP for back-office operations.

### How long does it take to implement a retail ERP?

SMB platforms like Brightpearl and Cin7 deploy in 4 to 8 weeks. Mid-market platforms like NetSuite and Acumatica take 3 to 6 months. Enterprise systems like Dynamics 365 Commerce, SAP, and Oracle Retail require 9 to 18 months, sometimes longer for global rollouts.

### Do I need separate systems for B2B and B2C?

Not necessarily. NetSuite, Dynamics 365, Acumatica, and Cin7 handle both within a single instance. The requirements for unified B2B/B2C include customer-specific pricing, payment terms, EDI compliance, and channel-specific fulfillment workflows. If your operations share inventory, a unified system eliminates reconciliation overhead.

### What ERP features matter most for marketplace sellers?

Real-time inventory sync to prevent overselling, automated order import and fulfillment routing, marketplace fee tracking at the SKU level, marketplace-specific returns processing, and listing management for consistent product data across channels. Brightpearl, Cin7, and NetSuite handle these natively.

## Final Considerations

The right ERP depends on three factors: your revenue scale, the channels you sell through, and how quickly you need to be operational. Enterprise systems from SAP and Oracle deliver unmatched depth but require significant time and capital. Mid-market platforms like NetSuite and Dynamics 365 balance capability with pragmatism. Purpose-built retail platforms like Brightpearl and Cin7 get you operational fastest but may require migration as you scale.

Start by mapping your channel architecture and transaction volumes. Shortlist two or three platforms that match your scale. Request scoped implementations from certified partners, insist on demos using your actual workflows, and talk to references in your industry segment. The ERP decision is a three-to-five year commitment. Invest the time to get it right.

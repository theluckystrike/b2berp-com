Food and beverage manufacturers face ERP requirements that general-purpose platforms handle poorly. Recipe management, catch weight processing, allergen tracking, FSMA compliance, lot-level traceability from receiving dock to retail shelf -- these are not features you bolt on with a connector and a custom field. Aptean and Infor both recognized this and built dedicated food and beverage ERP platforms. But they approach the market from different positions. Aptean is a food and beverage specialist built around the mid-market producer. Infor is an enterprise software company that built an industry-specific CloudSuite within a much broader portfolio. That distinction shapes everything from feature depth to pricing to implementation complexity.

## Executive Summary

Aptean wins for mid-market producers with 50 to 500 users who need deep food safety compliance, formula management, and lot traceability without enterprise overhead. Infor wins for larger enterprises with 200 to 5,000 users that need a broader platform, embedded AI for demand sensing, and AWS-hosted infrastructure scaling across multiple plants. Pricing ranges from $150 to $350 per user per month for Aptean and $200 to $400 per user per month for Infor. Implementation timelines run 4 to 10 months for Aptean and 6 to 14 months for Infor.

## Company Overview

### Aptean

Aptean is an enterprise software company focused on serving manufacturers and distributors in specialized industries. Food and beverage is its anchor vertical. Aptean Food & Beverage ERP (built on the former Schouw Informatisering and Ross ERP platforms) has served food producers for over 30 years, giving it institutional knowledge about how bakeries, dairies, meat processors, snack manufacturers, and beverage companies run their operations.

The platform was designed process-first. Recipe and formula management, batch production, potency tracking, catch weight handling, and shelf life management are native capabilities embedded in the core data model. Aptean serves approximately 4,000 food and beverage customers globally, with strength in North America, Europe, and Australia. The typical buyer is a mid-market food producer doing $20M to $1B in revenue, operating one to five production facilities.

### Infor

Infor is the third-largest enterprise software company globally, serving over 65,000 customers across 175 countries. Koch Industries acquired Infor in 2020 and funded a complete migration to Amazon Web Services. Infor CloudSuite Food & Beverage is one of approximately 12 industry-specific CloudSuites, each built on the Infor OS platform with shared infrastructure for analytics (Birst), AI (Coleman), and integration (ION).

CloudSuite Food & Beverage evolved from the Infor M3 platform, with deep roots in process manufacturing for Nordic and European food producers. The typical Infor buyer operates at $500M to $10B in revenue, runs 5 to 50 production facilities, and needs a platform consolidating ERP, warehouse management, supply chain planning, and workforce management under one technology stack.

## Food Safety and Compliance

Food safety compliance is non-negotiable in this industry, and both platforms treat it accordingly. The difference is in how they deliver compliance capabilities and at what scale they perform best.

### Aptean

Aptean Food & Beverage ERP was built around the assumption that every transaction might be audited. The platform provides pre-configured compliance templates for FSMA, HACCP, SQF, BRC, and GFSI-benchmarked standards. Critical control points are embedded directly into production workflows, meaning operators cannot bypass temperature checks, metal detection confirmations, or allergen changeover verifications without management override and documentation.

Aptean's compliance strength is its simplicity. A quality manager at a 200-person bakery can configure HACCP plans, define CCP monitoring points, and generate audit-ready reports without needing a consultant. Allergen tracking operates at the ingredient level, propagating allergen declarations through formulas to finished goods automatically.

### Infor

Infor CloudSuite Food & Beverage delivers the same regulatory frameworks but layers on enterprise-grade audit trail management, electronic signature support for FDA 21 CFR Part 11, and statistical process control (SPC) for quality analytics. Where Aptean excels at making compliance accessible to mid-market operators, Infor excels at making compliance scalable across large organizations with multiple regulatory jurisdictions. Coleman AI identifies quality trends before they trigger compliance events, and Birst analytics aggregates compliance KPIs across dozens of plants in real time.

## Recipe and Formula Management

Recipe and formula management is the functional core of any food and beverage ERP. Both platforms handle this well, but the depth and orientation differ.

Aptean provides multi-level formula management with automatic yield calculations, ingredient substitution logic, and nutritional analysis. Batch sizes scale based on available raw materials or production capacity. Formula versioning tracks every change with full audit history, and R&D teams can create experimental formulas in a sandbox before promoting to production.

Infor extends recipe management into a more sophisticated R&D workflow with advanced formula optimization -- the system suggests ingredient substitutions based on cost, availability, and nutritional targets. Integration with Infor PLM connects recipe development to packaging design, regulatory labeling, and new product introduction workflows. For companies managing thousands of active formulas, Infor's R&D integration reduces the time from concept to production.

## Traceability

Full chain traceability -- the ability to trace any finished product back to its raw material suppliers and forward to its retail destinations -- is a regulatory requirement under FSMA and an operational necessity for recall management.

Aptean delivers lot-level traceability with forward and backward tracking across every production stage. When a recall occurs, the platform identifies every finished product containing a specific raw material lot within seconds, plus every customer shipment that received affected product. Catch weight traceability is native, tracking both nominal and actual weights through the supply chain.

Infor matches Aptean's lot traceability and adds serialization for item-level tracking beyond lot granularity. ION integration connects supplier traceability data with internal production records and downstream distribution, giving visibility across multiple tiers of suppliers and co-manufacturers.

## Pricing

Aptean pricing falls between $150 and $350 per user per month depending on deployment model and modules. A 100-user deployment typically runs $200,000 to $400,000 annually. Implementation adds 0.8x to 1.5x first-year subscription value.

Infor pricing ranges from $200 to $400 per user per month, reflecting broader capabilities, AWS infrastructure, and embedded AI. A 300-user deployment typically costs $900,000 to $1,400,000 annually. Implementation adds 1.0x to 2.0x first-year subscription value.

The TCO gap widens when you factor in implementation. A mid-market producer spending $300K per year on Aptean with a $350K implementation reaches five-year TCO of approximately $1.85M. A larger enterprise spending $1.1M per year on Infor with a $1.5M implementation reaches five-year TCO of approximately $7.0M. Right-sizing the platform to your operation is the most consequential pricing decision.

## Implementation

### Aptean Implementation

Aptean ships with pre-configured industry processes for common food manufacturing scenarios, reducing the design phase. Typical timelines:

- **Core ERP (finance + production + quality):** 4-7 months
- **Full Suite (ERP + WMS + compliance + EDI):** 7-10 months
- **Multi-site rollout:** 9-14 months

Aptean's implementation consultants work exclusively in food and beverage, understanding seasonal production swings, co-packing relationships, and retailer compliance requirements natively. Consulting rates range from $150 to $250 per hour.

### Infor Implementation

Infor promotes accelerated deployment frameworks for CloudSuite Food & Beverage, with pre-built industry content reducing configuration effort. However, the broader platform surface area means more decisions and integration points. Typical timelines:

- **CloudSuite F&B (core):** 6-9 months
- **Full Suite with WMS + demand planning + AI:** 9-14 months
- **Multi-site / multi-entity global:** 14-24 months

Infor's ecosystem includes global SIs like Deloitte and Accenture alongside mid-market specialists. Consulting rates range from $175 to $325 per hour.

## Best For

**Choose Aptean Food & Beverage ERP if:**

- You are a mid-market food or beverage producer with 50 to 500 users
- Food safety compliance (FSMA, HACCP, SQF, BRC) is a primary driver for ERP selection
- You need native catch weight handling for meat, seafood, dairy, or produce
- Your formula management requirements are complex but your IT team is lean
- You operate one to five production facilities in a single regulatory jurisdiction
- Budget efficiency matters and you want deep food-and-beverage functionality without paying for enterprise capabilities you will not use
- You want an implementation team that speaks food manufacturing natively

**Choose Infor CloudSuite Food & Beverage if:**

- You are a large food and beverage enterprise with 200 to 5,000 users
- You need a platform that consolidates ERP, WMS, demand planning, and workforce management
- Coleman AI for demand sensing, predictive quality, and anomaly detection is strategically important
- You operate across multiple countries with different regulatory frameworks
- Your supply chain complexity requires network-level traceability beyond lot tracking
- You plan to invest in advanced analytics and want embedded Birst dashboards
- You need AWS-hosted infrastructure that scales elastically with production volume

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Aptean or Infor better for food safety compliance?

Both platforms provide robust food safety compliance for FSMA, HACCP, SQF, and BRC. Aptean is more accessible for mid-market organizations with smaller quality teams. Infor is stronger for enterprises across multiple regulatory jurisdictions, scaling across dozens of plants with FDA 21 CFR Part 11 support. The functionality is comparable; the difference is the scale at which each delivers it efficiently.

### Can Aptean handle catch weight processing?

Yes, catch weight (dual-unit) processing is a native capability in Aptean Food & Beverage ERP. The platform tracks both nominal and actual weights across purchasing, production, inventory, and sales transactions. This is essential for meat processors, seafood companies, and dairy operations where product weight varies by unit. Aptean's catch weight handling has been refined over decades of serving these verticals.

### Does Infor CloudSuite Food & Beverage include AI capabilities?

Yes. Infor embeds Coleman AI across the CloudSuite platform. For food and beverage operations, Coleman provides demand sensing that adjusts forecasts based on real-time signals, predictive quality analytics that identify trends before they cause compliance events, and anomaly detection across supply chain data. These capabilities require sufficient historical data to train, so organizations with fewer than two years of digital production records may not realize full benefit immediately.

### How long does it take to switch from one platform to the other?

Migrating between Aptean and Infor typically takes 10 to 20 months and costs 1.5x to 2.5x a greenfield implementation. The complexity comes from formula data migration, compliance record continuity, lot traceability history, and user retraining. Most manufacturers undertake this migration only when facing platform end-of-life, acquisition-driven ERP consolidation, or a fundamental shift in operational scale.

Choosing an ERP system when you employ 25 to 250 people on a manufacturing floor is a different exercise than selecting one for a 2,000-person enterprise. The evaluation criteria shift toward total cost of ownership, shop floor immediacy, and the realistic probability that your team will actually adopt the software. Global Shop Solutions and Epicor Kinetic both serve discrete manufacturers, but they approach the small manufacturer segment from opposite ends of the market. Global Shop Solutions was purpose-built for it. Epicor grew into it from a broader mid-market position. This comparison examines where each platform excels, where it falls short, and which type of small manufacturer should choose which system.

## Executive Summary

Global Shop Solutions is a Texas-based, employee-owned ERP vendor with over 3,000 customers, nearly all of them discrete manufacturers with 25 to 250 employees. The platform is an all-in-one system that bundles shop floor control, scheduling, inventory, quality, CRM, accounting, and business intelligence into a single integrated package. Epicor Kinetic is a much larger vendor serving approximately 28,000 customers across multiple industries, with a cloud-first architecture designed to scale from 50-employee shops up to 2,000-employee operations. For the small manufacturer evaluating both, the decision comes down to a trade-off between vertical depth and horizontal breadth. Global Shop Solutions delivers tighter shop floor integration and a lower total cost of ownership for smaller operations. Epicor offers stronger scalability, a more modern cloud architecture, and a wider partner ecosystem for companies that expect to grow beyond 250 employees within five years.

Pricing reflects this positioning. Global Shop Solutions typically runs $150 to $300 per user per month depending on module configuration and deployment method. Epicor Kinetic ranges from $175 to $350 per user per month, with cloud SaaS subscriptions at the higher end. Implementation timelines are comparable at 3 to 9 months for Global Shop Solutions and 4 to 12 months for Epicor, though Global Shop Solutions implementations tend to compress because the vendor handles deployment internally rather than through a channel partner network.

## Company Overview

### Global Shop Solutions

Global Shop Solutions has operated out of The Woodlands, Texas since 1976. The company is entirely employee-owned through an ESOP structure, which means it reinvests revenue into the product rather than servicing debt for outside investors. Global Shop Solutions serves over 3,000 customers in discrete manufacturing verticals including machine shops, metal fabrication, electronics assembly, plastics, and contract manufacturing. The company develops, implements, and supports its software entirely in-house with no channel partner network.

### Epicor Kinetic

Epicor Software Corporation has been building ERP platforms since 1972 and serves approximately 28,000 customers across manufacturing, distribution, retail, and building supply. Acquired by Clayton, Dubilier & Rice in 2020, the company accelerated investment in Epicor Kinetic, a cloud-native platform rebuilt with REST APIs, a low-code application studio, and multi-tenant cloud hosting. Epicor operates through direct sales and a network of value-added resellers, giving it broader geographic reach but introducing variability in implementation quality by partner.

## Shop Floor Features

This is where the comparison gets interesting for small manufacturers, because shop floor execution is where most ERP implementations succeed or fail in a production environment.

### Global Shop Solutions: Built for the Floor

Global Shop Solutions treats the shop floor as the center of its architecture rather than an afterthought bolted onto a financial system. The platform includes real-time data collection through barcode scanning and touchscreen kiosks positioned at each work center. Operators clock into jobs, report quantities, flag quality issues, and view work instructions without leaving their station. The scheduling engine uses finite capacity logic that accounts for machine availability, tooling constraints, labor skills, and material availability simultaneously. For a 50-person machine shop running 15 CNC machines across two shifts, this level of integration eliminates the Excel spreadsheets and whiteboard scheduling that most small manufacturers rely on before implementing ERP.

The quality management system is embedded directly into the production workflow. Inspection plans trigger automatically at defined operations, and non-conformance tracking follows the part through rework or scrap disposition without requiring a separate quality module login. Document control links revision-controlled drawings and work instructions to specific operations on the router, so operators always see the current revision.

### Epicor Kinetic: Broader but More Modular

Epicor Kinetic provides strong shop floor functionality through its Manufacturing Execution System, but the architecture is more modular than Global Shop Solutions. The base MES module handles job tracking, labor reporting, and work queue management. Advanced scheduling requires the Advanced Planning and Scheduling add-on, which adds finite capacity scheduling with constraint-based logic. For small manufacturers, this modular approach means the initial implementation may not include every shop floor feature out of the box, and each additional module increases the subscription cost.

Where Epicor pulls ahead is in mixed-mode manufacturing support. If your shop handles both discrete and repetitive or process manufacturing, Epicor can manage all three modes within a single system. Global Shop Solutions is optimized almost exclusively for discrete and job shop environments. Epicor also offers stronger integration with IoT data collection through its connected factory capabilities, which pull machine data directly from CNC controllers and PLCs without manual operator intervention.

## Pricing

Pricing in the ERP market is notoriously opaque, but the general ranges for these two platforms are well established through customer surveys and implementation consultant data.

**Global Shop Solutions** typically falls in the **$150 to $300 per user per month** range. The lower end reflects on-premise perpetual license arrangements amortized over five years with annual maintenance. The higher end reflects hosted cloud deployments with full support. Global Shop Solutions does not charge separately for individual modules in most configurations. The all-in-one pricing model means quality management, CRM, scheduling, and business intelligence are included rather than priced as add-ons. For a 30-user implementation, expect total first-year costs between $75,000 and $150,000 including implementation services.

**Epicor Kinetic** ranges from **$175 to $350 per user per month** for cloud SaaS subscriptions. On-premise perpetual licenses are still available but Epicor is actively steering customers toward SaaS. The modular pricing structure means the base ERP subscription covers core financials and operations, but advanced scheduling, advanced MES, quality management, and CRM may be priced as additional modules depending on the package tier. For a 30-user cloud implementation, expect total first-year costs between $120,000 and $200,000 including implementation, data migration, and training.

The cost differential narrows at larger user counts because Epicor offers volume discounts that Global Shop Solutions typically does not match.

## Implementation

### Global Shop Solutions

Implementation timelines typically run **3 to 9 months** depending on complexity. The single largest advantage is that Global Shop Solutions handles every implementation directly. There is no reseller in the middle. The team that scopes, configures, migrates data, trains users, and supports go-live works for Global Shop Solutions, eliminating the finger-pointing that plagues implementations split between a software vendor and a separate partner.

Because the system is all-in-one, there are fewer integration points to manage. The trade-off is that customization options are more limited than Epicor. Global Shop Solutions expects customers to adapt processes to the software's best practices rather than heavily customizing the platform.

### Epicor Kinetic

Epicor implementations typically run **4 to 12 months** for small manufacturers, with the timeline heavily dependent on which implementation partner is selected. Epicor's partner network includes hundreds of resellers with varying expertise levels. Selecting the right partner is arguably the most important decision in an Epicor project.

The Kinetic platform includes a low-code Application Studio for configuration and light customization without traditional development. However, this flexibility introduces risk. Over-customization during implementation is the leading cause of Epicor project delays and budget overruns in the small manufacturer segment.

## Scalability

This is where the two platforms diverge most sharply.

**Global Shop Solutions** performs exceptionally well for manufacturers with 25 to 250 employees operating from one or two facilities. The system handles complex job shop routing, high-mix low-volume production, and multi-level BOMs without performance issues at this scale. However, the platform shows limitations beyond 300 users or when manufacturers need multi-currency, multi-language, or geographically dispersed operations.

**Epicor Kinetic** was built to scale. The cloud-native architecture supports multi-site, multi-company, and multi-country operations within a single instance. A manufacturer that starts with 50 users can expand to 500 across five plants without re-implementing. If your five-year plan includes acquisitions, international expansion, or growing beyond 250 employees, Epicor provides a longer runway.

## Best For

### Choose Global Shop Solutions If:

- You operate a discrete manufacturing shop with 25 to 250 employees
- You want an all-in-one system with no surprise add-on module costs
- Shop floor data collection and real-time scheduling are your top priorities
- You prefer the vendor to handle implementation directly rather than through a reseller
- You value the stability and reinvestment priorities of an employee-owned company
- Your growth plan keeps you under 300 employees and two facilities for the foreseeable future
- You run a job shop, machine shop, fabrication shop, or contract manufacturing operation

### Choose Epicor Kinetic If:

- You have 50 or more employees and expect to grow beyond 250 within five years
- You need multi-site or multi-company support from day one
- Your manufacturing includes mixed-mode production (discrete, repetitive, and process)
- You want a cloud-native SaaS platform with automatic updates and no infrastructure management
- You need advanced IoT and connected factory capabilities for machine-level data collection
- Your industry requires specific compliance frameworks that Epicor's vertical solutions address
- You plan to integrate ERP with multiple external systems through robust APIs

## Verdict

For a small discrete manufacturer with 25 to 150 employees at a single facility, Global Shop Solutions is the stronger choice. The all-in-one pricing eliminates module stacking costs, direct implementation removes partner risk, and the shop floor integration is purpose-built for this exact environment. Employee ownership provides confidence the roadmap will continue serving small manufacturers.

For manufacturers with 100 to 250 employees who have growth ambitions, multi-site requirements, or mixed-mode production needs, Epicor Kinetic is the more future-proof investment. The higher initial cost is justified by the platform's ability to scale without a rip-and-replace migration in three to five years.

The overlap zone sits between 75 and 150 employees. Base the decision on growth trajectory rather than current size. Staying under 200 employees at one or two plants? Global Shop Solutions delivers more value per dollar. Planning to double in size or add facilities? Invest in Epicor now to avoid a painful migration later.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can Global Shop Solutions run in the cloud?**
Yes. Global Shop Solutions offers a hosted cloud option in addition to traditional on-premise deployment. The hosted option puts the system in Global Shop Solutions' data center with the vendor managing infrastructure, backups, and updates. It is not a multi-tenant SaaS architecture like Epicor Kinetic, but it eliminates the need for on-site servers.

**Does Epicor Kinetic work for job shops with fewer than 50 employees?**
Technically yes, but the cost structure and implementation overhead make it difficult to justify for very small operations. Epicor's sweet spot starts at 50 employees. Below that threshold, the per-user cost and implementation investment may exceed the ROI timeline that a smaller manufacturer can tolerate.

**Which system has better reporting and business intelligence?**
Global Shop Solutions bundles analytics and dashboards into the base subscription. Epicor offers Epicor Data Analytics as a more powerful BI tool, but it may cost extra depending on subscription tier. For standard production and financial reports, Global Shop Solutions provides more out of the box. For advanced analytics with custom data models, Epicor is more capable.

**How do support models differ?**
Global Shop Solutions provides direct support from its Texas headquarters. You call and reach a Global Shop Solutions employee. Epicor support quality depends on whether you purchased directly or through a reseller. Direct Epicor support is competent but can have longer response times. Reseller support varies significantly.

**Is data migration easier with one platform over the other?**
Complexity is similar. The differentiator is who manages it. Global Shop Solutions handles migration as part of its direct implementation with one team responsible end-to-end. Epicor migrations are typically managed by the implementation partner, adding a coordination layer.

**Can either system integrate with SolidWorks or other CAD platforms?**
Both support CAD integration with SolidWorks and AutoCAD. Epicor adds native Autodesk Inventor integration. Both synchronize bills of material and revision data between CAD and ERP, reducing manual BOM entry errors.

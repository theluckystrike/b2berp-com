Job shops operate under constraints that most ERP vendors do not fully understand. Every order is different. Routings change mid-run. Customers demand accurate quotes within hours, not days.

Cetec ERP and JobScope both target this segment, but they approach the problem from opposite directions. Cetec built a modern, cloud-native platform designed to make ERP affordable for small-to-mid-sized shops. JobScope, developed by Synergy Resources, took the specialist route: deep functionality for complex project manufacturing where cost tracking precision matters more than interface polish. This comparison examines both platforms across the dimensions that matter on the shop floor.

## Executive Summary

Cetec ERP is the better choice for job shops under 100 employees that want modern, browser-based ERP at a predictable price point. JobScope is the stronger platform for complex project manufacturers and defense subcontractors where granular cost tracking and earned value management are non-negotiable.

If your shop runs short-cycle jobs with standard routings, Cetec delivers 90% of what you need at a fraction of the cost. If your jobs span months, involve multiple subcontract operations, and require DCAA-compliant cost accounting, JobScope is purpose-built for that reality.

## Company Overview

### Cetec ERP

Cetec ERP is headquartered in Austin, Texas, and has been serving small-to-mid-sized manufacturers since 2011. The platform is entirely cloud-native and browser-based. There is no installed software, no terminal server, and no VPN requirement. Cetec has accumulated over 500 customers, primarily in the electronics contract manufacturing, machine shop, and precision fabrication segments.

Where legacy vendors charge six-figure implementation fees and multi-year commitments, Cetec offers month-to-month subscriptions starting at approximately $40 per user per month. The implementation timeline is measured in weeks rather than months, and Cetec handles data migration, configuration, and training internally.

The technical architecture is single-codebase multi-tenant SaaS. All customers run on the same version, updates roll out simultaneously, and there is no version fragmentation. The trade-off is limited customization.

### JobScope

JobScope is developed by Synergy Resources, a company that has focused exclusively on project-based and job shop manufacturing software for over three decades. The platform is designed for manufacturers that produce complex, engineered-to-order products where each job is essentially a unique project with its own bill of materials, routing, and cost structure.

JobScope's customer base includes aerospace subcontractors, defense manufacturers, medical device fabricators, and industrial equipment builders. These are environments where a single job can span six months, involve dozens of subcontract operations, and require detailed cost segregation for government contract compliance.

The platform is available as both a cloud-hosted solution and an on-premise deployment. JobScope pricing typically falls in the $150 to $300 per user per month range for cloud deployments, with on-premise licensing available for organizations that require it. Implementation timelines range from three to six months depending on complexity.

## Job Shop Specific Features

### Work Order Management

Cetec ERP handles work orders through a straightforward interface. You create a work order, define the routing with operations, assign work centers, and release to the floor. The system tracks labor and material against each operation in real time. For shops running 50 to 200 active jobs, this level of work order management covers the essentials without unnecessary complexity. Cetec also supports traveler documents that can be printed or displayed digitally at each workstation.

JobScope treats each work order as a project with potentially dozens of sub-assemblies, each with its own routing and material requirements. The multi-level work order structure allows a single customer order to cascade into fabrication orders, assembly orders, and subcontract orders, all linked and tracked as a unified project. This is critical when a single job involves 15 machined components feeding into 3 sub-assemblies that converge at final assembly.

### Routing and Travelers

Cetec provides configurable routings with operation sequences, setup and run times, work center assignments, and alternate routings with capacity calculations. Traveler documents are browser-printable and include operation details, inspection checkpoints, and material requirements.

JobScope extends routing into detailed process planning with simultaneous and sequential dependencies, overlap percentages, and crew size requirements. Outside processing operations within the routing automatically generate purchase orders when a job is released. JobScope travelers include engineering drawings, revision-controlled work instructions, and quality hold points with signature capture, which is essential for shops operating under AS9100 or ITAR requirements.

## Quoting and Estimating

Accurate quoting is the difference between profitable jobs and margin erosion. Both platforms include quoting modules, but the depth varies significantly.

Cetec ERP includes a built-in estimating tool that pulls from your rate tables, material costs, and historical job data. You can build a quote by selecting operations, entering quantities, and letting the system calculate based on your shop's actual cost structure. The quoting module integrates directly with the sales pipeline, so a won quote converts to a sales order and work order with a few clicks. For shops that quote 20 to 50 jobs per week with relatively standard operations, Cetec's estimating module is efficient and sufficient.

JobScope's estimating engine is parametric. You can define estimating models that calculate setup time, run time, and material requirements based on part characteristics like dimensions, material type, tolerance class, and surface finish requirements. This means a new quote for a part you have never made before can be estimated with reasonable accuracy if you have defined the parametric relationships. For shops bidding on complex assemblies where a single quote might involve 50 line items and 200 operations, this level of estimating sophistication pays for itself in quote accuracy alone.

JobScope also supports should-cost analysis, allowing you to compare estimated costs against actual costs from similar historical jobs. This feedback loop continuously improves estimating accuracy over time.

## Shop Floor Control

### Data Collection

Cetec ERP uses browser-based kiosk screens for shop floor data collection. Workers clock into operations using a tablet or shop floor PC with a simple scan-start-stop interface. Material transactions are also recorded at the kiosk, and there is no special hardware requirement beyond a browser.

JobScope supports dedicated shop floor terminals with barcode scanning, capturing labor, material, scrap, and rework transactions at the operation level. It also supports batch labor reporting for operations like heat treat or paint where multiple jobs are processed simultaneously.

### Real-Time Job Costing

This is where the platforms diverge most sharply. Cetec provides real-time job costing that shows actual vs. estimated cost at the job and operation level. You can see at any point during a job whether you are over or under budget on labor, material, and outside processing. For most small job shops, this level of visibility is a significant upgrade from spreadsheets or manual tracking.

JobScope's job costing goes deeper. The system tracks costs at the job, lot, serial number, and operation level. It supports multiple burden rates, overhead allocation methods, and cost pools. For defense subcontractors, JobScope provides DCAA-compliant cost accumulation with the audit trail that government contract accounting requires. The earned value management reporting shows schedule performance index and cost performance index for long-running jobs, giving project managers early warning of overruns.

## Pricing

Cetec ERP pricing starts at approximately $40 per user per month, with volume discounts available for larger deployments. Implementation is typically included in the subscription or priced as a modest one-time fee. There are no multi-year commitments required, and the month-to-month model means you can scale users up or down without contract renegotiation. A 25-user deployment at Cetec runs approximately $1,000 per month, or $12,000 annually.

JobScope pricing ranges from $150 to $300 per user per month for cloud deployments, depending on module selection and contract terms. On-premise licensing is also available, typically structured as a perpetual license plus annual maintenance. Implementation services are priced separately and can range from $30,000 to $100,000 depending on scope. A 25-user cloud deployment at the midpoint of $225 per user runs approximately $5,625 per month, or $67,500 annually.

The cost difference is not trivial. A small shop with 15 users pays roughly $7,200 per year for Cetec versus $40,500 or more for JobScope. The question is whether the additional functional depth justifies a 5x price premium, and for shops that genuinely need it, the answer is usually yes.

## Implementation

Cetec ERP implementations typically complete in two to six weeks. The process covers data migration, work center and rate table configuration, user training, and go-live support. Because the platform is standardized SaaS with no server provisioning required, Cetec's internal team handles the entire process.

JobScope implementations run three to six months. The process includes requirements gathering, process mapping, legacy data migration, custom report development, integration setup (EDI, CAD, accounting), user acceptance testing, and phased go-live. Organizations migrating from legacy systems with extensive historical data should budget for the longer end of this range.

## Best For

**Choose Cetec ERP if:**
- Your shop has fewer than 100 employees
- You run short-cycle jobs with relatively standard routings
- You want a modern, browser-based interface with minimal training overhead
- Budget predictability matters and you want to avoid six-figure ERP investments
- You need to be live in weeks, not months
- Mobile shop floor access is a priority
- You are replacing spreadsheets, QuickBooks, or a legacy system you have outgrown

**Choose JobScope if:**
- You manufacture complex, engineered-to-order products
- Jobs span weeks or months with multi-level assemblies
- You are a defense subcontractor requiring DCAA-compliant cost accounting
- Earned value management and project-level analytics are operational necessities
- You need parametric estimating for complex bid packages
- AS9100 or ITAR compliance drives your documentation requirements
- You have 20 or more users and can justify the higher per-user cost with functional depth

## Verdict

Cetec ERP and JobScope serve the job shop market from fundamentally different positions. Cetec democratizes ERP for small manufacturers with a capable system at a price point that eliminates the traditional barrier to entry. For the majority of small job shops, Cetec delivers meaningful operational improvement without the risk of a heavyweight deployment.

JobScope is not trying to be affordable or fast to implement. It is the most capable project manufacturing ERP for shops where cost tracking precision, regulatory compliance, and project complexity define operations. If your average job has five operations and ships in a week, JobScope is overkill. If your average job has 40 operations, three levels of sub-assembly, and a government customer requiring cost segregation by contract line item, JobScope is the right tool.

Match the tool to the problem, not the other way around.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Cetec ERP suitable for aerospace job shops?**
Cetec supports quality management and inspection tracking that can satisfy ISO 9001 requirements. However, shops operating under AS9100 or ITAR with stringent document control and traceability requirements may find JobScope's compliance-oriented features more complete out of the box.

**Does JobScope offer a cloud deployment option?**
Yes. JobScope is available as a cloud-hosted solution in addition to traditional on-premise deployment. The cloud version is hosted and managed by Synergy Resources, and pricing is structured on a per-user-per-month basis.

**Can Cetec ERP handle multi-level bills of materials?**
Cetec supports multi-level BOMs and can manage sub-assembly work orders within a parent job. However, for deeply nested assemblies with complex dependency chains, JobScope's project-oriented work order structure provides more granular control.

**What accounting integrations does Cetec ERP support?**
Cetec includes a built-in accounting module covering accounts receivable, accounts payable, and general ledger functions. It also integrates with QuickBooks for shops that prefer to maintain a separate accounting system.

**How does JobScope handle engineering changes mid-job?**
JobScope's document control system supports revision management with approval workflows. Engineering changes can be applied to active jobs with full traceability of which revision was in effect at each operation, which is critical for audit compliance in regulated industries.

**Is there a minimum user count for either platform?**
Cetec ERP does not impose a strict minimum and can accommodate shops with as few as five users. JobScope implementations typically start at ten to fifteen users due to the complexity and cost of implementation, though smaller deployments are possible.
